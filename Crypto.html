Shift-Ciphers

Shift ciphers is one of the simplest and most widely known encryption techniques. It is a type of substitution cipher in which each letter in the plaintext is replaced by a letter some fixed number of positions down the alphabet. Like if we take incrementer 3, then A will become D (A shifted 3 positions ahead.)

Affine-Ciphers

The affine cipher is type of monoalphabateic substitutioon cipher, where each letter in an alphabet is mapped to its numeric equivalent, encrypted using a simple mathematical function, and converted back to a letter. The fommula used means that each letter encrypts to one other letter , and back again, meaning teh cipher is essenctially a standard substitution cipher with a rule governing whic letter goes to which.
